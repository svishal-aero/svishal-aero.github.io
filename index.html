Vishal Srivastava
Hello there! I am Vishal, an aerospace engineer working at the intersection of data-driven methodologies, computational science, and fluid mechanics. While the tools I develop are relevant for several engineering disciplines, my research work primarily revolves around data-driven modeling of transition and turbulence in fluid flows. I graduated with a B.Tech. in Aerospace Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur in 2016, and a Ph.D. in Aerospace Engineering from the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor in 2022 (Go Blue!). During my Ph.D., I worked at the Computational Aerosciences Laboratory under the supervision of my advisor, Prof. Karthik Duraisamy, on creating methodologies to infer generalizable model augmentations for numerical models from limited data.

Currently, I work as a Senior Aerospace Engineer at Analytical Mechanics Associates where I collaborate with researchers at NASA Langley Research Center on data-driven transition and turbulence modeling. During my free time, I love testing new ideas in personal programming projects, reading fiction novels, and playing chess and video games.
